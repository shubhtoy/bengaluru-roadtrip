Bengaluru → Coorg → Ooty → Gokarna

The optimal 7-day loop — three two-night bases through coffee country, Nilgiri hills, and Arabian Sea beaches. Less driving, more living.

Why this version?

Most 7-day Bengaluru itineraries try to hit too many places — leading to 4+ hotel check-ins and half the trip behind the wheel. This version uses only 3 stops with 2 nights each, so you unpack once and actually explore. Mysuru isn't skipped — you stop for lunch and the palace — but you don't burn a night there.

💡 The math
3 check-ins vs 6 saves you ~6 hours of packing/unpacking/checkout admin. That's nearly a full extra day of experiences.

Day 1

Bengaluru → Mysuru → Coorg

Royal stopover then into the misty hills

🛣️ ~260 km ⏱️ 5–6 hrs (with stops) 🌡️ 15–25°C at Coorg
Morning: Mysuru (2–3 hr stop)
  • Mysore Palace — India's most-visited palace; arrive early (opens 10 AM) to beat crowds. ₹100 entry
  • Chamundi Hill — quick drive up for Chamundeshwari Temple + panoramic city views. See the 4.9m Nandi Bull on the way down
  • Lunch: try Mylari Dosa (legendary butter dosa since 1960) or Hotel RRR for a Mysuru thali
Evening: Arrive Coorg
  • Drive Mysuru → Madikeri (~120 km, 3 hrs through winding ghat roads)
  • Check into your coffee-estate homestay
  • Evening at leisure — unwind, enjoy estate dinner (Coorgi pandi curry, kadambuttu)

Food (lunch Mysuru + dinner)₹1,800

✔ Tip
Leave Bengaluru by 6:30–7 AM. The expressway gets you to Mysuru by 9:30. Explore till 1 PM, lunch, and reach Coorg by 5 PM sunset.
Day 2

Full day in Coorg

Waterfalls, coffee estates & sunset viewpoints

🛣️ ~50 km local ⛰️ 1,152 m elevation 🛏️ 2nd night, Coorg
What to see & do
  • Morning coffee estate walk — guided tour through robusta/arabica rows; learn the full bean-to-cup process; buy fresh roast
  • Abbey Falls — 70-ft cascade through coffee & spice plantations (best Sep–Dec after monsoon)
  • Raja's Seat — Madikeri's signature sunset viewpoint with landscaped gardens and valley vistas
  • Madikeri Fort — Tipu Sultan-era fort with a museum & temple inside
  • Omkareshwara Temple — unique blend of Islamic dome + Hindu temple architecture
  • Optional: Dubare Elephant Camp (morning, ~30 km) — bathe & feed rescued elephants
✔ The real Coorg
The magic is in the homestay itself — morning mist through coffee rows, homemade wine at dinner, kadambuttu with pork curry. Don't over-schedule this day.

Day 3

Coorg → Ooty

One of South India's most scenic drives — forests, ghats & tea

🛣️ ~237 km ⏱️ ~4 hrs ⛰️ Up to 2,240 m

The drive itself is a highlight — you'll pass through the Bandipur–Mudumalai wildlife corridor (watch for elephants!), then climb the famous 36-hairpin ghat road into the Nilgiris. The landscape transforms from dry deciduous forest into misty tea slopes.

Afternoon & evening in Ooty
  • Doddabetta Peak (2,637 m) — highest in the Nilgiris; telescope house + 360° views
  • Ooty Lake — pedal-boating; Rose Garden nearby
  • Botanical Garden — 22 acres, 1,000+ species, 20-million-year-old fossilised tree trunk
  • Tea factory tour — watch the full withering→rolling→drying process; free tasting
  • Chocolate shops on Commercial Road (King Star, Moddy's — buy in bulk!)
Mid-range resort₹5,000
Food₹1,500
Entries + fuel₹800
Day total~₹7,300
⚠️ Bandipur night ban
The Mysuru–Ooty road passes through Bandipur reserve — closed 9 PM–6 AM. Leave Coorg early enough to clear the forest by dusk.
Day 4

Ooty + Coonoor

Tea estates, viewpoints & the quieter Nilgiri charm

🛣️ ~40 km local 🛏️ 2nd night 🚂 Toy train!
What to see & do
  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway — UNESCO heritage rack-and-pinion steam train. The Coonoor→Ooty section is easiest to book (₹30 unreserved / ₹200 first class)
  • Dolphin's Nose — dramatic rock at 1,550 m; views of Catherine Falls plunging into the gorge below
  • Lamb's Rock — quieter viewpoint over Coimbatore plains
  • Sim's Park — 12-acre botanical garden in a ravine; 1,000+ species
  • Ketti Valley — "Switzerland of India"; stunning sunrise spot
  • Tea estate walk — many estates welcome visitors mid-morning
✔ Local wisdom
Base in Coonoor or Ketti Valley rather than Ooty town — quieter, greener, direct access to the best views without traffic.
Resort (same property)₹5,000
Food₹1,200
Train + entries₹400
Day total~₹6,600
ℹ️ Book the toy train early
Tickets sell out days ahead in peak season. Check IRCTC or the NMR counter at Coonoor station. The Mettuppalayam→Coonoor section (with steam loco) is the most iconic.
Day 5 · Long drive

Ooty → Gokarna

Mountains to coast — the biggest drive of the trip

🛣️ ~580 km ⏱️ 9–10 hrs 🏖️ Beach arrival

Route: Ooty → Gudalur → Bandipur (quick forest stretch) → Mysuru bypass → Hubballi → Gokarna. You'll pass through Bandipur's wildlife corridor on the descent — watch for elephants and gaur on the road.

⚠️ This is a marathon day
  • Leave Ooty by 5 AM. Clear Bandipur forest before the morning rush.
  • Breakfast in Mysuru area (~3 hrs from Ooty).
  • Highway from Hubballi onward is smooth 4-lane.
  • Arrive Gokarna by 4–5 PM for a sunset swim.
  • Fuel up the night before — sparse pumps on early stretches.
Why it's worth it
  • You wake up in misty hills and sleep with waves crashing — hard to beat that contrast
  • The Bandipur–Mudumalai morning drive is often full of wildlife
  • NH48 from Hubballi is a fast, well-maintained highway
Easier alternative
  • Swap the loop: do Bengaluru → Coorg → Gokarna → Ooty → Bengaluru (counter-clockwise). The Coorg→Gokarna leg is only ~350 km / 6–7 hrs, and Ooty→Bengaluru (via Mysuru) is just 270 km.

Day 6 · Beach day

Full day in Gokarna

Beach treks, water sports, sacred temples & legendary sunsets

🏖️ 5 beaches 🛶 Kayaking 🛏️ 2nd night
Beaches & activities
  • Om Beach — shaped like an ॐ; jet ski, banana boat, parasailing available
  • Kudle Beach — the relaxed one; cliff-side cafés, best sunset
  • Beach trek: Om → Half Moon → Paradise (2–3 hrs one way over headlands). Start before 9 AM, carry water, wear proper shoes
  • Kayaking — guided tour from Om Beach to hidden coves (~₹1,500/person)
  • Mahabaleshwara Temple — one of 7 mukti-sthalas; ancient Atmalinga. Dress modestly (no shorts)
Optional day trips
  • Murudeshwar (80 km) — world's 2nd tallest Shiva statue (37 m) on an ocean cliff
  • Yana Caves (50 km) — dramatic black limestone spires rising from dense forest
  • Mirjan Fort (24 km) — photogenic 16th-century laterite ruin
Beachside stay (2nd night)₹5,000
Food (seafood!)₹2,000
Water sports / kayak₹1,500
Day total~₹8,500
✔ Beach trek logistics
Trek to Paradise Beach (the quiet end) and take a ₹200 boat back to Om Beach. Saves your knees and you get a sea-level perspective of the cliffs you just climbed.
ℹ️ Where to eat
Namaste Café (Om Beach) for sunset drinks. Pai Hotel in town for authentic North Karnataka thali. Fresh grilled fish at any beach shack.
Day 7

Gokarna → Bengaluru

The long run home via NH48

🛣️ ~485 km ⏱️ 9–10 hrs 🏁 Home by evening

The NH48 route (Gokarna → Hubballi → Haveri → Davangere → Chitradurga → Tumkur → Bengaluru) is the fastest. Avoid the Sirsi ghat shortcut — narrow, slow, and tiring after a beach week.

✔ Final day game plan
  • Leave by 5 AM — catch sunrise on the coast road
  • Fuel up the night before (sparse early stretches)
  • Breakfast in Hubballi (good dhabas on the highway, ~2.5 hrs)
  • Lunch around Davangere/Chitradurga (~halfway)
  • Target arrival: 4–5 PM (before Bengaluru outer-ring evening rush)
  • Total tolls: ~₹600–800 (keep FASTag topped up)

Practical tips

🗓️ Best time

  • Oct–Mar: ideal — cool hills, dry coast
  • Sep–Oct: waterfalls peak; some ghat fog
  • Apr–May: hot plains, pleasant hills
  • Avoid Jun–Aug (heavy ghat rain)

🚗 Self-drive checklist

  • Car papers, DL, FASTag, insurance
  • Spare tyre (checked!), jack, basic toolkit
  • Offline maps — no signal in forests/ghats
  • Dashcam + phone mount + car charger

⚠️ Road rules

  • Bandipur: night ban 9 PM–6 AM
  • Expressway cameras: 100/120 strictly enforced
  • TN entry: green tax ₹150–300 may apply
  • Wildlife zones: honking banned; 30 km/h

🎒 Packing

  • Fleece/light jacket (hills drop to 8–12°C)
  • Rain jacket (ghats surprise year-round)
  • Walking shoes (trek + estate walks)
  • Swimwear + quick-dry towel
  • Modest clothing for temples

Counter-clockwise alternative

ℹ️ If 580 km on Day 5 is a dealbreaker

Flip the Gokarna/Ooty order:

Bengaluru → Coorg (2N) → Gokarna (2N) → Ooty/Coonoor (2N) → Bengaluru

  • Coorg → Gokarna: ~350 km / 6–7 hrs (way more manageable)
  • Gokarna → Ooty: ~580 km / 9 hrs (same long day, but you're rested after 2 beach days)
  • Ooty → Bengaluru: ~270 km / 5 hrs via Mysuru (easy finish!)

This version ends with the shortest drive home — you arrive refreshed instead of exhausted.
