The Perfect Week on the Road from Bengaluru

Three two-night bases through coffee country, Nilgiri mountains and Arabian Sea beaches. Less driving, more living.

Why this route?

Most 7-day Bengaluru trips cram 5–6 places and burn half the holiday driving. This version uses 3 stops × 2 nights each — you unpack once per place and actually explore. Mysuru is a lunch-stop, not an overnight.

💡 The simple math
3 check-ins vs 6 saves ~6 hours of packing/checkout admin. That's nearly an extra day of real experiences.

01

Bengaluru → Mysuru → Coorg

Royal stopover, then into misty coffee hills

🛣️ ~260 km⏱️ 5–6 h with stops🌡️ 15–25 °C Coorg
Morning — Mysuru (2–3 h stop)
  • Mysore Palace — most-visited palace in India. Opens 10 AM, ₹100 entry. Go early to beat queues
  • Chamundi Hill — quick drive; Chamundeshwari Temple + city panorama + monolithic Nandi Bull on descent
  • Lunch: Mylari Dosa (legendary since 1960) or Hotel RRR for Mysuru thali
Afternoon — to Coorg
  • Mysuru → Madikeri: 120 km, 3 h via winding ghat roads
  • Arrive coffee-estate homestay by sunset
  • Dinner: pandi curry (Coorgi pork), kadambuttu (rice dumplings), bamboo-shoot pickle

Expressway length117 km access-controlled + 21 km Mysuru ring. ~1.5 h if no exits.

Full day in Coorg

Waterfalls, coffee walks & spice-estate life

🛣️ ~50 km local⛰️ 1,152 m elevation🛏️ 2nd night Coorg
Things to do
  • Morning estate walk — guided tour through robusta/arabica; bean-to-cup process; buy fresh roast
  • Abbey Falls — 70 ft cascade through coffee & spice plantations
  • Raja's Seat — Madikeri's signature sunset point with landscaped gardens
  • Madikeri Fort — Tipu Sultan-era fort; quirky museum & temple inside
  • Omkareshwara Temple — unique Islamic dome + Hindu temple hybrid
  • Optional AM: Dubare Elephant Camp (~30 km) — feed & bathe elephants

03

Coorg → Ooty

One of South India's most spectacular drives

🛣️ ~237 km⏱️ ~4 h⛰️ Climbs to 2,240 m

The road transforms from dry deciduous forest into mist-wrapped tea slopes. You cross the Bandipur–Mudumalai wildlife corridor (elephants cross the road!) then climb the famous 36-hairpin ghat into the Nilgiris.

Afternoon in Ooty
  • Doddabetta Peak (2,637 m) — highest Nilgiris; telescope house + 360° views
  • Ooty Lake — pedal-boating; Rose Garden adjacent
  • Botanical Garden — 22 acres, 1,000+ species, 20M-year-old fossil tree
  • Tea factory — withering→rolling→drying demo; free tasting
  • Chocolate shops on Commercial Road (King Star, Moddy's)
⚠️ Bandipur night ban
NH766 through Bandipur: closed 9 PM–6 AM. Also 30 km/h limit inside the reserve, no honking. Leave Coorg by 7–8 AM.

05

Ooty → Gokarna

Mountains to coast — the big drive

⚡ Longest day🛣️ ~580 km⏱️ 9–10 h🏖️ Sunset arrival

Route: Ooty → Gudalur → Bandipur → Mysuru bypass → NH48 → Hubballi → Gokarna.

📋 Route research (Team-BHP + Wanderlog verified)
Total distance578–607 km depending on exact route (Wanderlog: 377 mi / Rome2Rio: 578 km direct)
Drive time8.5–10 hrs realistic (includes fuel stops, 30 km/h Bandipur zone)
Best routeOoty → Gudalur → Gundlupet → Mysuru bypass → Hubballi → Ankola → Gokarna. NH48 from Hubballi is excellent 4-lane.
AvoidSirsi ghat section — narrow, slow. Team-BHP drivers recommend Hubballi–Yellapur–Ankola instead.
Fuel strategyFull tank at Ooty. Refuel at Nanjangud/Mysuru area. Next stop: Hubballi. Don't rely on pumps in Bandipur.
NH48 condition"Post Hubli till Tumakuru, bliss to drive, easily 90 km/h" — Team-BHP Oct 2025 report.
⚠️ Day 5 game plan
  • Leave Ooty 5 AM. Clear Bandipur by 7–8 AM (wildlife on road at dawn!)
  • Breakfast: Mysuru area (~3 h). Lunch: Hubballi (~6 h). Both have good dhabas.
  • NH48 Hubballi onward: smooth, fast, well-lit.
  • Arrive Gokarna 4–5 PM → sunset swim at Kudle Beach.
✔ Why it's worth it
You wake shivering in the hills and fall asleep to crashing waves. Dawn through Bandipur is magical for wildlife. And the contrast recharges you.

Full day in Gokarna

Beach treks, water sports & sacred temples

🏖️ 5 beaches🛶 Kayak🛏️ 2nd night
Beaches & activities
  • Om Beach — ॐ-shaped; jet ski, banana boat, parasailing
  • Kudle Beach — cliff cafés, best sunset point
  • Beach trek: Om → Half Moon → Paradise (2–3 h one way)
  • Kayaking — Om Beach to hidden coves (~₹500–1,500/person)
  • Mahabaleshwara Temple — 7 mukti-sthalas; ancient Atmalinga

Practical tips

🗓️ Best time

  • Oct–Mar: ideal (cool hills, dry coast)
  • Sep–Oct: waterfalls peak; ghat fog
  • Apr–May: hot plains, pleasant hills
  • Avoid Jun–Aug (heavy ghat rain)

🚗 Self-drive checklist

  • DL, RC, insurance, FASTag
  • Spare tyre (checked!), jack, toolkit
  • Offline Google Maps — no signal in forests
  • Dashcam · phone mount · car charger

⚠️ Road rules

  • Bandipur: night ban 9 PM–6 AM
  • Expressway: speed cameras (100/120)
  • TN entry: green tax ₹150–300
  • Wildlife: no honking, 30 km/h

🎒 Packing

  • Fleece (hills: 8–12 °C nights)
  • Rain jacket (ghats year-round)
  • Walking shoes (trek + estates)
  • Swimwear + quick-dry towel
  • Modest temple clothing

Counter-clockwise alternative

If the 580 km Day 5 is a dealbreaker, flip the order.

The "easy finish" version

Bengaluru → Coorg (2N) → Gokarna (2N) → Ooty (2N) → Bengaluru
  • Coorg → Gokarna: ~350 km / 6–7 h (much shorter)
  • Gokarna → Ooty: ~580 km (same long day, but you're rested after 2 beach days)
  • Ooty → Bengaluru: ~270 km / 5 h via Bandipur & Mysuru (easiest drive home!)

This ends with the shortest leg home. You arrive fresh.
