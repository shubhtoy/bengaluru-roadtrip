Three two-night bases through coffee country, the Nilgiri mountains, and pristine Arabian Sea beaches. Less driving, more living.

Why this route works

Most 7-day itineraries from Bengaluru try to hit 5–6 places and end up with half the trip spent in the car. This version uses only 3 stops with 2 nights each — you unpack once and actually explore.

💡 The math that matters
3 check-ins instead of 6 saves you ~6 hours of packing/checkout admin over the week. That's almost a full extra day of actual experiences.

01

Bengaluru → Mysuru → Coorg

Royal stopover, then into misty coffee country

🛣️ ~260 km total ⏱️ 5–6 hrs with stops 🌡️ 15–25°C at Coorg
Morning — Mysuru (2–3 hr stop)
  • Mysore Palace — most-visited palace in India; arrive when it opens at 10 AM. ₹100 entry
  • Chamundi Hill — quick drive up; Chamundeshwari Temple + city panorama. See the monolithic Nandi Bull on the descent
  • Lunch: Mylari Dosa (legendary since 1960) or Hotel RRR for a Mysuru thali
Afternoon — Drive to Coorg
  • Mysuru → Madikeri: ~120 km, 3 hrs through winding ghats
  • Check into your coffee-estate homestay by sunset
  • Evening at leisure — Coorgi dinner: pandi curry, kadambuttu, bamboo shoot pickle
✔ Timing
Leave Bengaluru by 6:30 AM. The expressway gets you to Mysuru by 9:30. Explore & lunch till 1:30 PM. Reach Coorg by 5 PM for sunset.

02

Full day in Coorg

Waterfalls, coffee walks & spice-estate life

🛣️ ~50 km local ⛰️ 1,152 m elevation 🛏️ 2nd night, Coorg
Things to do
  • Morning estate walk — guided tour through coffee & pepper rows; bean-to-cup process; buy fresh roast direct
  • Abbey Falls — 70-ft cascade through plantations; best Sep–Dec post-monsoon
  • Raja's Seat — Madikeri's signature sunset viewpoint with landscaped gardens
  • Madikeri Fort — Tipu Sultan-era fort with museum & temple inside
  • Omkareshwara Temple — unique Islamic dome + Hindu temple hybrid
  • Optional AM: Dubare Elephant Camp (~30 km) — bathe & feed rescued elephants
✔ Don't over-schedule
The magic of Coorg is the homestay itself — morning mist through coffee rows, homemade wine at dinner, the silence. Leave space to just be.

03

Coorg → Ooty

One of South India's most spectacular drives

🛣️ ~237 km ⏱️ ~4 hours ⛰️ Climbs to 2,240 m

The road transforms from dry deciduous forest into mist-wrapped tea slopes. You'll cross the Bandipur–Mudumalai wildlife corridor (watch for elephants crossing!) before the famous 36-hairpin climb into the Nilgiris.

Afternoon & evening in Ooty
  • Doddabetta Peak (2,637 m) — highest in the Nilgiris; telescope house + 360° views
  • Ooty Lake — pedal-boating; Rose Garden nearby
  • Botanical Garden — 22 acres; 1,000+ species; 20-million-year-old fossilised tree trunk
  • Tea factory tour — watch withering→rolling→drying; free tasting
  • Chocolate shops on Commercial Road (King Star, Moddy's)

Food₹1,500

⚠️ Bandipur night ban
NH766 through Bandipur is closed 9 PM–6 AM. Leave Coorg early to clear the forest in daylight.
04

Ooty + Coonoor

Tea estates, toy trains & valley viewpoints

🛣️ ~40 km local 🚂 Toy train day! 🛏️ 2nd night
Things to do
  • Nilgiri Mountain Railway — UNESCO heritage; rack-and-pinion steam. Coonoor→Ooty short section easiest to book (₹30 unreserved / ₹200 FC)
  • Dolphin's Nose — dramatic rock at 1,550 m; view of Catherine Falls plunging into the gorge
  • Lamb's Rock — overlooking Coimbatore plains; quieter alternative
  • Sim's Park — 12-acre botanical garden in a ravine
  • Ketti Valley — "Switzerland of India"; stunning sunrise
  • Tea estate walk — many welcome visitors mid-morning
✔ Local secret
Base in Coonoor or Ketti Valley rather than Ooty town — quieter, greener, and you skip the traffic chaos.
Resort (same property)₹5,000
Food₹1,200
Train + entries₹400
Day total~₹6,600
🎫 Book the toy train early
Sells out days ahead in season. Check IRCTC or the NMR counter at Coonoor station. The Mettuppalayam→Coonoor section (with steam loco) is the most iconic.
05

Ooty → Gokarna

Mountains to coast — the big drive day

⚡ Longest day 🛣️ ~580 km ⏱️ 9–10 hrs 🏖️ Beach arrival

Route: Ooty → Gudalur → Bandipur corridor → Mysuru bypass → Hubballi → Gokarna. You'll descend through wildlife forest at dawn (elephants are common!) and hit smooth highway from Hubballi onward.

⚠️ Strategy for the long day
  • Leave by 5 AM. Clear Bandipur forest before traffic builds.
  • Breakfast: Mysuru area (~3 hrs from Ooty)
  • Lunch: Hubballi (~6 hrs). NH48 onward is fast 4-lane.
  • Arrive Gokarna: 4–5 PM — just in time for a sunset swim at Kudle Beach.
  • Fuel up the night before (sparse pumps on early stretches).
Why it's worth it
  • You wake up shivering in hills and fall asleep to crashing waves — hard to beat
  • Dawn through Bandipur is magical for wildlife sightings
  • NH48 from Hubballi is well-maintained 4-lane
Easier alternative

Flip it: Bengaluru → Coorg → Gokarna → Ooty → Bengaluru (counter-clockwise). Coorg→Gokarna is only ~350 km, and Ooty→Bengaluru is just 270 km home. See the alternative box below.

Full day in Gokarna

Beach treks, water sports, temples & seafood

🏖️ 5 beaches 🛶 Kayaking 🛏️ 2nd night, Gokarna
Beaches & activities
  • Om Beach — ॐ-shaped; jet ski, banana boat, parasailing
  • Kudle Beach — cliff cafés, the best sunset in Gokarna
  • Beach trek: Om → Half Moon → Paradise (2–3 hrs one way). Start before 9 AM, carry water, wear shoes not flip-flops
  • Kayaking — guided from Om Beach to secret coves (~₹1,500/person)
  • Mahabaleshwara Temple — one of 7 mukti-sthalas; ancient Atmalinga. Dress modestly
Optional day trips
  • Murudeshwar (80 km) — 37 m Shiva statue on an ocean cliff
  • Yana Caves (50 km) — black limestone spires in dense forest
  • Mirjan Fort (24 km) — photogenic 16th-century laterite ruin
Beach stay (2nd night)₹5,000
Food (fresh seafood!)₹2,000
Water sports / kayak₹1,500

✔ Trek tip
Trek to Paradise Beach and take a ₹200 boat back to Om Beach. Saves knees + you see the cliffs from sea level.

07

Gokarna → Bengaluru

The long run home via NH48

🛣️ ~485 km ⏱️ 9–10 hrs 🏁 Home by evening

Take the NH48 (Gokarna → Hubballi → Haveri → Davangere → Chitradurga → Tumkur → Bengaluru). Avoid the scenic-but-slow Sirsi ghat shortcut on the final day.

✔ Final day game plan
  • Leave by 5 AM — catch sunrise on the coast road
  • Fuel up the night before
  • Breakfast: Hubballi (~2.5 hrs)
  • Lunch: Davangere/Chitradurga (famous for Davangere benne dosa)
  • Target: arrive by 4–5 PM (beat Bengaluru outer-ring rush)
  • Total tolls: ~₹600–800

Practical tips

🗓️ Best time to go

  • Oct–Mar: ideal — cool hills, dry coast
  • Sep–Oct: waterfalls at peak; some ghat fog
  • Apr–May: hot plains, pleasant hills
  • Avoid Jun–Aug (heavy ghat rain, poor visibility)

🚗 Self-drive checklist

  • Car papers, DL, FASTag, insurance copy
  • Spare tyre (pressure checked!), jack, toolkit
  • Offline Google Maps — no signal in forests
  • Dashcam · phone mount · car charger

⚠️ Key road rules

  • Bandipur: night ban 9 PM–6 AM
  • Expressway: speed cameras (100/120 zones)
  • Tamil Nadu entry: green tax ₹150–300 may apply
  • Wildlife zones: no honking, 30 km/h limit

🎒 What to pack

  • Fleece/light jacket (hills: 8–12°C at night)
  • Rain jacket (ghats surprise year-round)
  • Walking shoes (trek + estate walks)
  • Swimwear + quick-dry towel for Gokarna
  • Modest clothes for temples (cover shoulders)

Counter-clockwise alternative

If the 580 km Day 5 feels like too much, flip the Gokarna and Ooty order.

The "easy finish" version

Bengaluru → Coorg (2N) → Gokarna (2N) → Ooty / Coonoor (2N) → Bengaluru
  • Coorg → Gokarna: ~350 km / 6–7 hrs (much more manageable)
  • Gokarna → Ooty: ~580 km / 9 hrs (same long day, but you're rested after 2 beach days)
  • Ooty → Bengaluru: ~270 km / 5 hrs via Mysuru (the easiest drive home!)

This version ends with the shortest drive home — you arrive fresh instead of drained.
